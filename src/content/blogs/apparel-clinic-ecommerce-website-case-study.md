## The Ecommerce Challenge

Fashion customers make decisions from imagery, product information, sizing, availability, delivery expectations, and trust. The website must keep those details easy to scan without allowing decorative effects to interrupt product discovery.

The project also needs an operational foundation. Product updates, categories, promotions, policies, and support information change after launch, so maintainability matters as much as the first visual impression.

## UX Priorities

### Mobile product discovery

Navigation, category paths, product cards, and primary actions should remain clear on narrow screens. Touch targets need stable dimensions and text must fit without crowding.

### Product information hierarchy

The interface should help customers find the product name, imagery, price or buying action, options, description, care or material details, delivery information, and relevant policies in a predictable order.

### Trust before checkout

Visible contact information, policies, secure payment cues, and consistent brand presentation reduce uncertainty. Trust should come from accurate information rather than unsupported badges or urgency counters.

## SEO and Performance Framework

Important ecommerce checks include descriptive product and category URLs, unique titles, product copy, image alt text, internal links, canonical handling, product structured data where applicable, compressed media, and stable image dimensions.

The main performance targets are LCP under 2.5 seconds, INP under 200 milliseconds, and CLS below 0.1 at the 75th percentile of real visits. These targets should be monitored using field data after enough traffic is available.

## What Success Should Measure

1. Product discovery from category to item.
2. Add-to-cart or primary buying actions.
3. Checkout starts and completed orders.
4. Mobile errors or abandonment points.
5. Organic visibility for useful categories and products.
6. Support questions that indicate missing information.
