## What Should You Check Before Hiring?

Start with the company's own website and portfolio. A credible website design provider should demonstrate clear navigation, responsive layouts, readable content, fast loading, and working contact paths. Ask whether the examples were designed and developed by the same team and what part of each project they handled.

Then review the written proposal. It should state the number of pages, included features, content responsibility, revision limits, SEO scope, hosting responsibility, delivery process, and post-launch support. Avoid making a decision only from the lowest quoted price.

## Website Design and Development Should Work Together

Website design covers visual hierarchy, layout, brand presentation, readability, and user experience. Website development turns that design into a working website with responsive behavior, forms, integrations, performance, and technical SEO.

A design may look attractive in an image but still fail on a phone, load slowly, or make enquiries difficult. The best result comes when design and development are planned together around a measurable business goal.

## SEO Should Begin Before Launch

An SEO-ready website needs more than a plugin. The team should plan:

- One clear purpose for every important page
- Search-friendly titles and meta descriptions
- Logical H1 and H2 headings
- Descriptive URLs and internal links
- Useful service and location content
- Image compression and alt text
- Canonical tags, sitemap, and crawl controls
- Relevant structured data
- Fast mobile performance

SEO cannot guarantee a ranking, but a clean foundation avoids expensive rework after the website is live.

## Questions to Ask a Website Design Company

Ask these questions before paying an advance:

1. What pages and features are included?
2. Who writes and approves the content?
3. Will the website be mobile-responsive?
4. What on-page and technical SEO work is included?
5. Who owns the domain, hosting, design files, and website?
6. How are revisions and delays handled?
7. What support is available after launch?
8. Can the website be expanded later?

Clear answers make proposals easier to compare.
