## Direct Answer

**A small business website usually takes 1 to 3 weeks once requirements and content are ready. A larger SEO-focused website may take 4 to 8 weeks, while ecommerce stores, redesigns, and custom platforms can take 6 to 16 weeks or more.**

The schedule depends less on the number of calendar days promised and more on scope clarity, content readiness, decision speed, integrations, and testing.

## Typical Website Timelines

| Project type | Planning timeline | Main dependencies |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Campaign landing page | 3-7 working days | Approved offer, copy, form, tracking |
| 5-page business website | 1-3 weeks | Content, logo, images, feedback |
| SEO-focused service website | 3-6 weeks | Keyword map, service content, schema |
| WordPress website | 2-6 weeks | Theme approach, plugins, migration |
| Small ecommerce store | 4-8 weeks | Products, payment, shipping, policies |
| Website redesign | 4-10 weeks | Content audit, redirects, migration |
| Custom web application | 6-16+ weeks | Workflows, data, APIs, user testing |

These ranges assume timely feedback. Complex integrations or large content migrations can extend them.

## The Six Main Project Stages

### 1. Discovery and scope

The team defines goals, users, pages, features, content ownership, integrations, timeline, and exclusions. A rushed scope usually creates expensive changes later.

### 2. Sitemap and content planning

Important services receive dedicated pages. The team maps headings, calls to action, FAQs, proof, local intent, and internal links before visual work begins.

### 3. Wireframes and design

Wireframes establish page hierarchy. Visual design then applies typography, colour, spacing, imagery, buttons, forms, and responsive behaviour.

### 4. Development

Approved layouts become working pages. Forms, content management, ecommerce, analytics, schema, and integrations are added according to scope.

### 5. Quality assurance

The website is checked on common mobile and desktop sizes. Links, forms, metadata, accessibility basics, images, redirects, and browser behaviour are tested.

### 6. Launch and monitoring

The production domain, SSL, analytics, sitemap, robots rules, forms, and backups are verified. Search Console and real-user performance data can then guide improvements.

## What Speeds Up a Website Project?

1. Approve a written sitemap and feature list before design.
2. Assign one decision-maker for consolidated feedback.
3. Prepare service facts, contact details, policies, and images early.
4. Give specific feedback linked to the business goal.
5. Freeze major scope changes after development starts.
6. Provide product data in a consistent spreadsheet for ecommerce.
7. Confirm domain, hosting, email, and third-party account access.

## What Commonly Causes Delays?

"Make it more premium" is not actionable until the team defines what premium means for the audience. Late feature additions, missing product images, unapproved copy, repeated layout changes, and unavailable API access can stop several stages at once.

A realistic plan includes client review time. Three rounds of feedback delivered over three weeks cannot fit inside a one-week build, even if development itself is fast.

## Example: Faridabad Business Website

A Faridabad service company needs Home, About, six service pages, two location pages, a blog, contact form, WhatsApp, FAQs, and basic SEO. A practical schedule might allocate three days for discovery and content mapping, one week for design, one to two weeks for development, and several days for review and launch checks.

If all content is supplied late, the same project takes longer. If the team starts with approved factual copy, the schedule becomes more predictable.

## Can AI Make Website Development Instant?

AI can accelerate research, first drafts, code suggestions, image preparation, and testing ideas. It does not remove the need to verify business facts, design a coherent experience, secure integrations, test forms, review accessibility, or make accountable launch decisions.

The best use of AI reduces repetitive work while keeping human review at every customer-facing step.

## Final Recommendation

Ask for a milestone schedule, not only a final date. Each milestone should identify the deliverable, reviewer, feedback window, and dependency. This makes delays visible early and protects quality.
